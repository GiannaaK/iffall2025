About Ochazuke

Ochazuke (お茶漬け) is a simple, comforting Japanese dish of rice topped with tea. A humble, home-style meal — warm, nostalgic, Customizable

Dietary Notes & Warnings

  • Allergies: Seafood (salmon, cod roe) common. Soy & gluten often in broth/soy sauce.
  • Vegan/Vegetarian: Often uses bonito (fish) for broth and seafood toppings.
  • Low-Sodium: Soy sauce and cod roe increase salt content.
  • Caffeine Sensitivity: Green tea contains caffeine.

Ingredients (1 Serving)

  • 1 cup Dashi
  • ½ cup green tea
  • 1 teaspoon sake
  • 1 teaspoon mirin
  • 1 teaspoon Nitto Jozo White Tamari
  • 2 teaspoons Suehiro Usukuchi Soy Sauce
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 2 oz broiled salmon
  • 1 umeboshi, pit removed and roughly chopped
  • 2 teaspoons thinly sliced scallions
  • Jacobson Furikake, to taste

Instructions

1

In a small saucepot combine the dashi, green tea, sake, mirin, white tamari, and soy sauce. Bring to a simmer.

2

In a serving bowl large enough for rice and broth, add the steamed rice and pour the hot dashi/tea broth over it.

3

Place the baked salmon in the center of the bowl.

4

Add the topping of your choice. Chopped umeboshi next to the salmon, and place the sliced scallions on the opposite side (optional).

5

Sprinkle with Jacobson furikake and enjoy immediately while warm.

Optional: Customize with shredded nori, different fish, tofu, or daikon sprouts. For raw fish, slice thinly and pour hot tea/dashi to gently cook.
